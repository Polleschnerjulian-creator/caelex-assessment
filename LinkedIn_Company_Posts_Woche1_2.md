## POST 1 — DIENSTAG Woche 1: Regulatory Reality Check

**Format:** Text + Infografik (1080x1080)
**Bild:** `post1_6_regimes.png`

### Post-Text:

A European space startup in 2026 has to navigate 6+ regulatory regimes simultaneously.

Here they are:

1. National Space Laws — 10 different licensing frameworks across Europe. France alone requires EUR 60M in liability insurance.

2. NIS2 Directive — In force since October 2024. Space operators are classified as "essential entities." Penalties: up to EUR 10M or 2% of global revenue.

3. ITAR/EAR Export Controls — A single US-made component puts an entire satellite under US jurisdiction. No de minimis exception.

4. ITU Spectrum Coordination — Filing fees from CHF 5,710 to CHF 116,000 per satellite system. Miss a deadline, lose the frequency.

5. Debris Mitigation — FCC's 5-year deorbit rule is in effect since September 2024. It applies to non-US satellites too.

6. Mandatory Insurance — Varies by jurisdiction. No insurance, no license.

How do most operators manage all of this today? Excel spreadsheets and EUR 600/hour law firms.

Caelex maps every regime, every jurisdiction, every requirement — in one platform.

What's the biggest regulatory challenge your team faces? We'd love to hear from operators, founders, and compliance teams.
