# Caelex Scholar — Legal Compliance Gap Analysis & Implementation Roadmap

**Scope:** `caelex.eu/scholar` — free, university-licensed (B2B2C), SSO-gated legal-research database. Operator: Caelex (German sole proprietorship / Kleinunternehmer §19 UStG, "Inhaber: Julian Polleschner", Berlin). Stack: Next.js 15 on Vercel; Neon PostgreSQL in eu-central-1 (Frankfurt). AI feature: semantic search over OpenAI embeddings. Audience: EU university students/staff (some may be minors). Corpus: space-law sources (mostly public legal text) with a 600-char cap on closed-licence ITU/ISO text.

**Code baseline:** verified against worktree `.worktrees/caelex-scholar`, branch `feat/caelex-scholar`, HEAD `d699f457`. Scholar UI in `src/app/(scholar)/scholar/`, API in `src/app/api/scholar/`, logic in `src/lib/scholar/`.

> **THIS IS NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Items are tagged **[CLAUDE-TECH]** (a developer can implement now), **[CLAUDE-DOC]** (a developer can draft a template, but it **needs qualified-lawyer review** before publishing/binding), or **[LAWYER]** (binding legal text or a classification call that requires counsel). Every `[CLAUDE-DOC]` legal text is non-binding until a qualified lawyer signs off.

## 1. Applicable Legal Frameworks

| Framework                                                                        | Why it applies to Scholar                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **GDPR / DSGVO** (Reg. 2016/679)                                                 | Scholar processes personal data of EU data subjects (account, search history, bookmarks/reading lists, preferences, masked-IP login events).                                                                                                                                                              |
| **BDSG** (German Federal Data Protection Act)                                    | Caelex is established in Germany; BDSG §38 lowers the DPO threshold; §26 covers employee/applicant data.                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| **Controller/Processor split — GDPR Art. 28 / 26**                               | B2B2C model: the licensing **university is controller**, Caelex is **processor** for the contracted service AND **controller** for its own product/security/AI decisions (dual role).                                                                                                                     |
| **ePrivacy Directive Art. 5(3) → German TDDDG §25** (ex-TTDSG, renamed May 2024) | Any read/write of the user's device (cookies, localStorage for prefs/history) needs opt-in consent unless strictly necessary — applies regardless of whether data is personal.                                                                                                                            |
| **§ 5 DDG** (Digitale-Dienste-Gesetz, ex-§5 TMG since 14 May 2024)               | Imprint obligation for the commercial digital service.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **EU AI Act** (Reg. 2024/1689)                                                   | Scholar's semantic search is an AI system "interacting with natural persons" → Art. 50 transparency (from 2 Aug 2026); Art. 4 AI-literacy already in force (2 Feb 2025).                                                                                                                                  |
| **EAA (Dir. 2019/882) / BFSG** (in force 28 Jun 2025)                            | Accessibility regime for digital services. **"Free" does NOT exempt.** But Caelex likely escapes via (a) **microenterprise exemption** (<10 staff AND ≤€2M) — services are _fully_ exempt; and (b) the **consumer-contract trigger** (B2B2C → arguably no Verbrauchervertrag). **[LAWYER must confirm.]** |
| **Copyright — § 5 UrhG (DE) / Decision 2011/833/EU**                             | Official legal texts are gemeinfrei (favourable); validates the corpus reuse.                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Sui generis Database Right (Dir. 96/9/EC Art. 7)**                             | Even when items are public, a third-party _curated compilation_ is protected against substantial extraction — provenance discipline needed.                                                                                                                                                               |
| **Closed-licence standards (ITU / ISO-IEC)**                                     | ISO/IEC own full copyright; the 600-char cap is the mitigation.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| **TDM — Arts. 3 & 4 DSM Dir. → §§ 60d, 44b UrhG**                                | Building embeddings = TDM; commercial use under §44b must respect machine-readable opt-outs for protected sources.                                                                                                                                                                                        |
